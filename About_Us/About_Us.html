The Dhyansport Institute of Sports & Physical Education Research is the academic and research arm of the DhyanSport Sports & Physical Education Foundation, a Section 8 non-profit organization registered under the Companies Act, 2013.Established with a vision to transform the landscape of sports education and wellness in India, the Foundation serves as the backbone for institutional initiatives that bridge traditional physical culture with modern sports science. It is committed to promoting inclusive education, scientific research, professional training, and community outreach across diverse domains such as physical education, fitness, rural sports, and allied health sciences.

Key highlights of the Foundation...

  • Publishing the Indian Journal of Sports Studies (ISSN 2229-3558) ‚Äî a national platform for scholarly dialogue in sports and physical education.
  • The Indian Society of Sports Scientists, dedicated to research, training, and academic collaboration.
  • The Indian Association of Sports Anthropometry, focusing on human measurement, biomechanics, and indigenous physical practices.
  • Running academic programs and training modules in partnership with recognized bodies like NCTE, NSDC, UGC, SAI, and Khelo India.
  • Supporting community-based outreach and grassroots development, especially in rural and underserved regions.

üìú Legal Status

  • Section 8 Company: The Foundation is incorporated as a Section 8 Company (Non-Profit Organization), registered under the Companies Act, 2013 in India. This gives the Foundation legal recognition, eligibility for tax exemptions, and credibility in its activities.
  • Registered Address: The official address of the Foundation for legal documents, correspondence, and notifications from government authorities or the public.
  • Registrar of Companies (RoC): The government body overseeing company registrations, which issues the certificate of incorporation making the Foundation a legal entity.
  • Tax Exemptions and 80G/12A Registration: The Foundation applies for 80G (donation exemptions) and 12A (recognition as a charitable trust) registration with the Income Tax Department.
  • Other Licenses and Approvals: Depending on activities, additional approvals or registrations may be required, including for publishing or hosting events.
  • Partnerships and Compliance: MOUs with institutions, government bodies, and global networks outline roles, responsibilities, and legal commitments.
